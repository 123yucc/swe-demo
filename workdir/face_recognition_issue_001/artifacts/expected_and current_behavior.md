"Title: Face Detector struggles with low-resolution images due to static parameters.\n\n## Description:\n\nThe current face detection implementation in `FaceDetector` uses fixed parameters for `min_face_size` and detection confidence. This static configuration makes it inefficient when processing low-resolution images or images where faces occupy a small portion of the frame, leading to frequent missed detections.\n\n## Current behavior:\n\nThe `FaceDetector` class initializes its MTCNN model with a `min_face_size` of 40 pixels and applies a fixed confidence threshold of 0.9 during post-processing. This means any face smaller than 40x40 pixels or detected with a confidence score below 0.9 is ignored. Consequently, in images with low overall resolution or small faces, these critical features are often overlooked, resulting in a failure to detect faces even when visibly present to a human observer. The system does not adapt its sensitivity to the input image quality.\n\n## Expected behavior:\n\nAfter implementing the proposed changes, the `FaceDetector` should dynamically adjust its internal MTCNN parameters or perform intelligent pre-processing based on image characteristics and user-defined sensitivity settings. For example, if `set_detection_sensitivity(min_face_size_factor=0.5)` is called, the effective minimum face size should be reduced (e.g., to 20 pixels) allowing for the detection of smaller faces. Similarly, adjusting the `confidence_threshold_offset` should modify the required confidence for detection. This adaptive behavior will significantly improve the success rate of face detection in challenging scenarios, ensuring that faces in low-resolution images or small faces within a frame are reliably identified, thus enhancing the overall robustness and accuracy of the face recognition system.\n"
